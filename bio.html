bio

I'm a cognitive neuroscientist finishing my PhD at Emory University, where I study how aging changes the brain's navigation systems using neuroimaging and virtual reality. Over the past six years, I've been lucky enough to build something from the ground up—a navigation research arm that combines VR technology, advanced brain imaging, and reproducible science.

I'm a proud Lebanese-American, born to a family that values connection, food, and resilience. In everything I do, I think globally and work to build inclusive, collaborative spaces in neuroscience.

Currently, I lead a team of 15+ researchers (undergrads, grad students, postdocs) and manage a study with 110+ participants. I've established concurrent TMS-fMRI as an institutional capability at Emory, expanding access to cutting-edge methodology for the broader Atlanta neuroscience community. I also served as Co-President of Graduates in Neuroscience, where I led a 22-member board representing 120+ PhD students.

Before Emory, I earned my BS in Neuroscience at Georgia Institute of Technology, where I graduated with Highest Honors as a Zell Miller Scholar. My path into research started with curiosity about how the brain creates our sense of space and has grown into a passion for building tools and teams that push the field forward.

Beyond research, I care deeply about how science is done, shared, and who gets to participate. I'm passionate about mentorship—I've directly mentored 15+ trainees who've gone on to win awards, complete honors theses, and secure competitive fellowships. I work on improving research infrastructure, from creating open-source analysis pipelines to establishing better organizational systems for graduate student communities.

Outside the lab, you'll find me making music with my band la brat, exploring Atlanta's food scene (always on the hunt for the best Lebanese food), or experimenting in the kitchen.
