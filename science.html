science

I study how the brain builds mental maps of the world — and what changes as we age. My work combines brain stimulation, neuroimaging, and virtual reality to understand the neural mechanisms behind spatial navigation. I develop open-source tools that bring naturalistic, real-world complexity into the lab, and I contribute to international efforts to advance methods in cognitive and clinical neuroscience.

Spatial Navigation Aging Virtual Reality

Distinct aging-related profiles of allocentric knowledge recall following navigation in an immersive, naturalistic, city-like environment

"Not all older adults lose their sense of direction the same way."

Using NavCity, our immersive VR city, we compared how younger and older adults (N=60) form world-centered mental maps after navigating a novel environment. We identified distinct aging-related profiles — some older adults retained strong map-like representations while others relied more heavily on body-centered cues, pointing toward more personalized approaches to assessing spatial cognition in aging populations.

2025 · Preprint (PsyArXiv) · doi.org/10.31234/osf.io/daq64 First Author

Spatial Navigation Open-Source Tool Virtual Reality

Formation of allocentric representations after exposure to a novel, naturalistic, city-like, virtual reality environment

"What if you could watch someone build a mental map — from scratch?"

We built NavCity — an open-source, immersive virtual-reality city — and paired it with NARA, a novel assessment that measures how well people form world-centered maps. Repeated exposure improved navigation and tracked with stronger map-like knowledge. NavCity and NARA provide the research community with standardized tools to study how humans learn to navigate new environments.

2025 · Neuropsychologia · doi.org/10.1016/j.neuropsychologia.2025.109290 First Author

TMS-fMRI Clinical Neuroscience

Consensus guidelines for the use of concurrent TMS-fMRI in cognitive and clinical neuroscience

"What happens when you stimulate the brain and image it at the same time?"

Concurrent TMS-fMRI is one of the most powerful tools for establishing causal links between brain regions and behavior. As part of a large international collaboration, I contributed to the clinical use cases section of these consensus guidelines, covering best practices for experimental design, data acquisition, and analysis.

Functional Connectivity Sleep Disorders

Altered functional connectivity and spatiotemporal dynamics in individuals with central disorders of hypersomnolence

"Why doesn't napping help some people feel rested?"

This study used fMRI to compare brain connectivity in people with idiopathic hypersomnia, narcolepsy type 1, and healthy controls — both at rest and after napping. Each condition showed a distinct neural signature, with the hypersomnia group showing reduced connectivity in key brain networks, advancing our understanding of why excessive sleepiness persists despite sleep.
